Markdown math

Posted on 2018-10-16 | In Markdown

Writing math formula with markdown KATEX. This blog also contains some knowledge writing $\TeX{}$, which can help you to write math formula with markdown easily.

Begin with

use single dollar to show the math formula within the row

$e^{i*\pi} +1 = 0$ is a beautiful equation

use double dollar to show the formula in next row

if a is the hypotenuse length of a right triangle with right angle length b and c, then

$$a^2 = \sqrt{b^2 + c^2}$$

For more symbols, please refer to Symbols and Functions, Function support in KATEX

Super-sub scripts and tags

use ^ and _ to show superscripts and subscripts respectively, if you want to include more than one characters, use {...} to group them. Also you can use \tag{...} at the begining to add tags of the formula

$$\tag{this is a tag}f(x) = \int^{+\infty}_{-\infty}e^{-x}dx $$

Square root

The square root is entered as \sqrt; the $n^{th}$ root is generated with \sqrt[n]. If just the sign is needed, use \surd.

$$\sqrt[3]{2} $$

Fraction

\frac{...}{...}, This style is obtainable
in text style with \tfrac. The reverse, i.e. display style fraction in text is made with \dfrac.

the pdf of normal distribution N~($\mu$,$\delta$):

$$f(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}\delta}e^{-\frac{(x-\mu)^2}{\delta^2}}$$

Dots type

dots code
$\cdot$ \cdot
$\ldots$ \ldots
$\vdots$ \vdots
$\ddots$ \ddots

Under-over line/braces

The commands \overline and \underline create horizontal lines directly over or under an expression

$$0.\overline{3} = \underline{\underline{1/3}}$$

The commands \overbrace and \underbrace create long horizontal braces over or under an expression

$$\underbrace{\overbrace{(a+b+c)}^6 \cdot \overbrace{(d+e+f)}^7}_\text{meaning of life} = 42$$

Hat and tilde

$$f(x) = x^2 \qquad f’(x) = 2x \qquad f’’(x) = 2 $$

$$ \hat{XY} \quad \widehat{XY} \quad \bar{x_0} \quad \bar{x}_0$$

Wide hats and tildes covering several characters are generated with \widetilde and \widehat. Notice the difference between \hat and \widehat and the
placement of \bar for a variable with subscript

Delimiters

Regular ones

Round and square brackets can be entered with the
corresponding keys and curly braces with \{, all other delimiters are generated with special commands (e.g. \updownarrow).

If you put \left in front of an opening delimiter and \right in front of a closing delimiter, LATEX will automatically determine the correct size of the delimiter.

$$1 + \left(\frac{1}{1-x^{2}} \right)^3 \qquad \left. \ddagger \frac{~}{~}\right) $$

Large ones

using the commands \big, \Big, \bigg and \Bigg as prefixes to most delimiter commands

$$\Big((x+1)(x-1)\Big)^{2} $$

$$\big( \Big( \bigg( \Bigg( $$

$$\big| \Big| \bigg| \Bigg| $$

$$\big\Downarrow \Big\Downarrow \bigg\Downarrow \Bigg\Downarrow $$

Stack symbols

$$f_n(x) \stackrel{*}{\approx} 1$$
$$\sum^n_{\stackrel{0<i<n}{j\subseteq i}}$$

Bold Symbols

use \mathbf, mathematical symbols are normally italic, and doesn’t work on lower case Greek letters

$\mathbf{\mu}$, $\mathbf{M}$

use pmb or bm. (bm does not work on the hexo blog)

$\pmb{\mu}$, $\pmb{A}$, $\boldsymbol{I}$

Such method can also be used to make letters bold

Vectors

This is done with the \vec command. The two commands
\overrightarrow and \overleftarrow are useful to denote the vector

$$ \vec{a} \qquad \vec{AB} \qquad \overrightarrow{AB} $$

Arrays and Matrices

Arrays

use the array environment, with \\\\ command to break lines.

$$
\begin{array}{}
x_1 & x_2 & \ldots \\
x_3 & x_4 & \ldots \\
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots
\end{array}
$$

the {} behind {array} denotes the format of elements, you can add r,c,l(right center left) and other format you like, for example, the code below shows the first and the second element are centered and separate with the third element with | which is also centered

$$
\left[
\begin{array}{cc|c}
1 & 2 & 3 \\
4 & 5 & 6
\end{array}
\right]
$$

Matrix

use & to separate elements in one row, and \\\\ to separate rows

simple matrix

$$
\begin{matrix}
a & b \\
c & d
\end{matrix}
$$

matrix with delimiter

change the parameter in \begin{...}, \end{...}. If we note the matrix form with parameter matrix as A then other parameters can be shown like this:

parameter symbol
matrix A
pmatrix (A)
bmatrix [A]
Bmatrix {A}
vmatrix det(A)
Vmatrix norm(A)

examples:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & 3 \\
4 & 5 & 6 \\
7 & 8 & 9
\end{bmatrix}
$$

matrix with ellipsis

$$
\left[
\begin{matrix}
x_{11} & x_{12} & \cdots & x_{1n} \\
x_{21} & x_{22} & \cdots & x_{2n} \\
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
x_{n1} & x_{n2} & \cdots & x_{nn} \\
\end{matrix}
\right]
$$

Cases and align

Cases

See a case

$$
f(x) = \begin{cases}
1 &\text{if } x \in Q \\
0 &\text{if } x \not\in Q
\end{cases}
$$

Align

use & to denote the location of align, use \\ to change a row.

$$
\begin {aligned}
A&=B \\
&=C \\
&=D
\end {aligned}
$$

Functions

Names of functions are often typeset in an upright font, and not in italics as variables are, $\LaTeX$ provide some of these common function names:

$$ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\sin x}{x}=1 $$

For functions missing from the list, use the \DeclareMathOperator command. But we cannot do that here as the KATEX cannot support euqation environment right now. There are many things that the current KATEX cannot support. Hope the developers release the new version ASAP.

binomial distribution
